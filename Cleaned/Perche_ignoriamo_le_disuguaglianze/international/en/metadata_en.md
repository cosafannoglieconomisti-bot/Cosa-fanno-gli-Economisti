# Video Metadata - Why Do We Ignore Inequality?

## YouTube Description
The study "Fair Shares and Selective Attention" by Amasino, Pace, and van der Weele, published in the American Economic Journal: Microeconomics in 2024, explores how visual attention influences our perception of merit and luck. Through large-scale experiments, the researchers demonstrate that those in a position of advantage tend to ignore data on real merit to justify their status, while those who are disadvantaged focus more on performance net of luck. These results suggest that selective attention is a key mechanism behind the polarization of opinions on economic inequality.
