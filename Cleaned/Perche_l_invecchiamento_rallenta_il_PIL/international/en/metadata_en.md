# Metadati Video - Why Does Population Aging Slow Down GDP? (EN)

## Descrizione YouTube
The study "The Effect of Population Aging on Economic Growth, the Labor Force, and Productivity" by Maestas, Mullen, and Powell, published in the American Economic Journal: Macroeconomics in 2023, analyzes the unprecedented impact of demographic aging on U.S. economic growth. Researchers found that every 10% increase in the share of the population over age 60 reduces GDP per capita by 5.5%. Surprisingly, two-thirds of this slowdown is not due to a shrinking workforce, but rather to a decline in labor productivity affecting all age groups.
